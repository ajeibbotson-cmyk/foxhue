Section 01

The Brief

We set out to design a website for ourselves — one that reflects who Foxhue is as a studio and positions us clearly in the market. The challenge was finding a design approach that communicates creative capability while remaining approachable to the small and medium-sized businesses we work with.

“Design a website for Foxhue that establishes us as a serious creative studio without alienating the SME clients who need us most. It should feel like the work of a studio that sweats the details — because we do.”

  • Professional but approachable — creative authority without pretension or corporate sterility
  • SMB-focused — clear value proposition for businesses that may never have worked with a design agency before
  • Distinctive in a crowded market — stand apart from template-driven web agencies and marketing-first competitors
  • Portfolio-driven — let the work speak, with case studies that demonstrate process and outcomes
  • Locally rooted — authentically positioned in Bicester, Oxfordshire, not pretending to be a London agency
Section 02

Research Phase

Before sketching a single layout, we studied 12 award-winning agency and studio websites across different styles and market positions. Our goal was to understand what makes a studio website genuinely effective — not just visually impressive, but commercially persuasive.

Methodology

We focused on sites that had received recognition from Awwwards, CSS Design Awards, or Lovie Awards, filtering for agencies of a similar size and ambition to Foxhue. We were not looking at the massive global agencies with 200-person teams — we were studying studios that do exceptional work at a boutique scale.

For each reference site, we documented:

  • Layout approach and visual hierarchy decisions
  • Typography choices and how they communicate positioning
  • How the studio presents its work and tells client stories
  • The balance between aesthetic ambition and commercial clarity
  • Motion, interactivity, and loading patterns

From this research, three distinct creative threads emerged — each one viable, each with a different personality. These became the foundation for our three design directions.

Section 04

Design Principles

Four guiding principles emerged from the research — consistent threads that appeared across the strongest reference sites regardless of their specific aesthetic. These principles informed all three design directions.

Typography Strategy

DM Serif Display paired with Inter. The serif heading font communicates premium creative capability and editorial confidence. The sans-serif body font ensures readability and approachability for SME audiences who need clarity over style. This pairing says: we are serious about craft, and we are easy to work with.

Colour Approach

Deep navy primary for authority. Vibrant coral accent for energy and distinctiveness. Near-white background for breathing room. This palette is distinctive without being aggressive — it stands out in a market dominated by teal, royal blue, and corporate grey without alienating conservative business owners.

Motion Philosophy

Subtle, purposeful motion only. Every animation must communicate something — drawing attention to a key message, revealing content in a logical sequence, or providing feedback on interaction. Following the principle established by Stokt and Anyflow: motion as communication, never as decoration.

Case Study Format

A consistent four-part structure: client challenge, Foxhue’s approach, design evolution, and business results. This format educates potential clients on how we work while providing the evidence that builds trust. Inspired by Grafico’s educational case studies and boxChilli’s metrics-driven approach.
